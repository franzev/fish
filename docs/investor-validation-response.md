# FISH Product Validation Response

## A defensible revision, evidence plan, and investor-readiness gate

Prepared 15 July 2026 in response to *FISH Product Validation - Brutal Startup Assessment*.

## Executive decision

The assessment is substantially correct about the original three-module concept. FISH should not defend that concept and should not claim that copy changes have validated it. The strongest response is a narrower company:

> FISH is the calm, coach-led follow-through hub for neurodivergent professionals improving workplace English. After each coaching session, the coach assigns one practical communication action. The client sees one next step, practices or responds without shame, and keeps one-to-one context with the coach.

This revision removes the general habit app, open social network, coaching marketplace, proprietary video platform, automatic confidence scoring, microphone surveillance, exact-time smartwatch promise, and universal wellness routine from the initial business.

The launch ICP is one segment: a United States-based independent workplace-English communication coach with 10-40 active adult professional clients, at least five of whom self-report ADHD-related follow-through difficulty. The initial user is one of those existing adult clients. Executive-function coaches, general English tutors, employers, clinics, universities, and clients outside the United States are excluded from the first cohort. FISH is initially sold as coach-enablement software, not as a medical treatment, consumer wellness subscription, or marketplace.

The United States is the recommended validation geography because the assessment, pricing, and initial legal analysis are U.S.-oriented. This is a planning decision, not evidence that the segment is reachable. Before recruitment, the founder must document a credible U.S. coach-access channel; if that cannot be shown, the geography must be deliberately reselected and the legal, pricing, and market sections reworked before any data is collected.

### What is true today

- The repository has useful foundations: role-based authentication, client profiles, coach/client relationships, messaging, booking, calling, accessibility preferences, and a shared UI kit.
- The current product rule, "coach-first, code-second," is directionally correct and should govern the next milestone.
- Demand, willingness to pay, outcome improvement, retention, market size, founder right-to-win, and independence from a single coach are not yet proven.
- Privacy tooling is incomplete and must precede any public pilot involving sensitive profile or coaching information.

### Honest verdict

The revised thesis is credible enough to validate and materially stronger than the assessed concept. It is not yet evidence-ready for institutional investment. A perfect document score can be earned by answering every concern and defining falsifiable gates; an investable company score can only be earned with paid pilots, retained behavior, comparative outcomes, privacy readiness, and repeatable acquisition.

The requested investor verdict becomes defensible when the gates in this document are passed. Until then, the correct verdict is:

> Investable hypothesis; not yet an investable result.

## 1. The one narrow business

### Customer and job

**Initial buyer:** a U.S.-based independent workplace-English communication coach with 10-40 active adult professional clients, at least five of whom self-report ADHD-related follow-through difficulty.

**Initial user:** one of the coach's existing adult professional clients who self-reports ADHD-related follow-through difficulty. FISH does not require or verify a diagnosis.

**Painful job:** preserve momentum between sessions without asking the client to navigate a course catalog, remember scattered assignments, or tolerate repeated generic reminders.

**Current workaround:** chat threads, email, worksheets, calendar reminders, shared documents, and manual coach chasing.

**Five-minute value:** a coach assigns one action; the client opens FISH and immediately sees that action, its context, and one clear way to respond.

### Product loop

1. In or after a session, the coach assigns one concrete workplace-English action.
2. The client sees one next step. There is no template gallery or plan picker.
3. The client sees one primary response, "Done." A secondary "Can't today" disclosure offers "Blocked," "Pause," or "Ask coach" without presenting five competing actions. Optional text can add context. Audio capture, upload, transcription, and storage are disabled for the entire initial validation cohort.
4. The coach receives the response in the existing one-to-one context and adjusts the next action.
5. Before the next session, both parties see a short factual recap without a score, grade, or broken streak.

### First workflow to validate manually

Use one action type in the initial study: a coach-assigned workplace email draft due in two to seven days. The coach and client agree the real communication context; the coach follows the pre-registered assignment-quality rubric. Interview answers and meeting contributions are excluded from the primary cohort so task difficulty and completion meaning do not drift. FISH must not automate the technique until the manual proof gate passes.

### Initial scope

Keep:

- Auth and roles.
- Client profiles with minimum necessary data.
- One-to-one coach/client context.
- One assigned next action.
- A low-friction client response using status and optional text only.
- Booking links or existing lesson booking where already useful.
- Calm return-after-a-gap behavior.
- Export, deletion, retention, and consent controls before public use.

Pause or remove from the investor story:

- Open community feed and professional social network.
- Friends/discovery as a growth thesis.
- Marketplace of coaches.
- Proprietary video, whiteboard, captioning, or transcription as differentiation.
- Habit library, morning routine, mantras, lip trills, and hundreds of exercises.
- AI confidence scores or claims about emotional regulation.
- Background or "invisible" microphone use.
- Exact-time smartwatch alarms and custom cross-platform haptics.
- Clinical or neuroscience-flavored claims.

Existing infrastructure does not need to be deleted immediately, but it must not drive roadmap or positioning. Public exposure of community capabilities should remain off until separately validated and staffed for safety.

### Page 3 - Three startups, broad segment, and non sequitur

**Concern 1:** Anchor, School, and Reef are three startups.

**Solution:** Remove the module architecture from the deck. The product is one coach-to-client loop. Internal capabilities are named by their job, not aquatic metaphors.

**Concern 2:** "Neurodivergent adults" is not a usable initial segment.

**Solution:** Use the launch ICP consistently: U.S.-based independent workplace-English communication coaches with 10-40 active adult professional clients, at least five of whom self-report ADHD-related follow-through difficulty. Executive-function coaching, general tutoring, employer sales, and other neurodivergent segments are explicit later expansion hypotheses. Diagnosis is not required.

**Concern 3:** Unsupported hours do not prove prompts work.

**Solution:** Replace causal certainty with a falsifiable hypothesis and compare personalized coach-assigned actions with the client's current reminder method.

**Replacement language:**

> Some neurodivergent professionals in workplace-English coaching struggle to carry one agreed practice action into the week. We are testing whether a single coach-assigned next step, kept in the same one-to-one context, increases completion without increasing anxiety or monitoring pressure.

### Page 4 - Unsupported mechanism, unwanted routine, crowded field

**Concern 1:** Haptics and neuroscience language do not prove improved discipline, regulation, or coaching outcomes.

**Solution:** Delete "digital dopamine crash," "interest-based neural loop," "ground the nervous system," "proven morning exercise," and any claim that coaching fails. Use only observable product behavior and measured outcomes.

**Concern 2:** Lip trills, mantras, animations, praise, early-morning framing, and haptics may be embarrassing, inaccessible, or infantilizing.

**Solution:** No universal routine. A coach may assign a technique only after validating it with that client. The default response can be completed silently, with neutral copy, at any schedule. Missed days do not reset progress. Sensory and timing controls are optional and minimal.

**Concern 3:** Tiimo, Inflow, Finch, Focusmate, and Shimmer cover adjacent needs.

**Solution:** FISH does not compete as a planner, generic ADHD content library, self-care pet, body-doubling service, or coaching marketplace. It differentiates through a narrow operating workflow: coach-assigned workplace-English action plus retained one-to-one context for neurodivergent clients. This remains a positioning hypothesis until customers choose it over their current workaround.

**Replacement language:**

> FISH does not ask clients to select a program or manage another planner. Their coach assigns one context-specific communication action; FISH makes that action immediately visible and keeps the response with the coaching relationship.

### Page 5 - Defensibility, PWA limits, and microphone trust

**Concern 1:** "Gentler" and "all in one" are not defensibility.

**Solution:** Do not claim a moat today. Build potential defensibility from four compounding assets: a coach distribution network; a codified, coach-validated assignment protocol; longitudinal outcome data collected with consent; and workflow integration that makes FISH the system of record for between-session coaching. The feature itself is copyable.

**Concern 2:** PWA/watch promises are overstated and onboarding is too complex.

**Solution:** Remove wearables from the core promise. Use web notifications only as optional, noncritical convenience. Never promise exact delivery. Default pilot onboarding is a direct secure link with no Home Screen installation, watch configuration, or microphone permission.

**Concern 3:** "Invisibly listens" is unacceptable.

**Solution:** Delete the phrase and the behavior. Audio capture, upload, transcription, and storage are disabled for the initial cohort. Any later audio exercise is a separate product phase and requires a clear user action, persistent recording indicator, purpose-specific consent, immediate playback/delete choice, defined storage and backup deletion, a jurisdictional review, and an accessibility/bias evaluation. Background capture is prohibited.

### Page 6 - False confidence metrics and sensitive data

**Concern 1:** Pitch, duration, and volume do not measure confidence or emotional regulation and may discriminate.

**Solution:** No confidence percentage. For the pilot, use self-report and transparent facts only:

- Primary action: Done.
- Secondary disclosure: Can't today.
- Within that disclosure: Blocked, Pause, or Ask coach.
- Optional text reason.

The resulting metric is **client-reported agreed-action completion**, not objective skill improvement. Coach-confirmed action relevance, coach-rated session preparedness using a fixed rubric, client-rated usefulness, and naturally occurring text artifacts are separate secondary measures. FISH makes no English-proficiency or communication-improvement claim without a separate validated outcome study.

If audio is later validated for a specific English-speaking exercise, show only measurements whose meaning is plain, such as recording duration or number of attempts. Do not infer confidence, mental state, diagnosis, fluency, or treatment effect.

**Concern 2:** Diagnosis, voice, transcripts, coaching notes, habits, and employment context create privacy and breach risk.

**Solution:** Adopt a minimum-necessary data policy:

- Do not require a diagnosis.
- Do not accept or store raw audio during the initial cohort.
- Do not record calls during the initial cohort.
- Keep health and accessibility disclosures private to the client unless they explicitly share them with a named coach.
- Separate consent for profile sharing, product analytics, and research. Audio/transcription consent belongs only in a future, separately approved audio phase.
- Publish a data inventory, retention schedule, subprocessors list, deletion/export process, access-control matrix, incident response plan, and jurisdictional review before public pilot.
- Use Supabase RLS for authorized reads and command functions for sensitive writes; verify policies with live tests.
- Obtain legal advice on the FTC Health Breach Notification Rule, state privacy laws, and recording consent. Do not claim "HIPAA compliant" unless applicability and controls are independently established.

The FTC states that many health apps outside HIPAA can still fall under the Health Breach Notification Rule, including the July 2024 amendments. See [FTC guidance](https://www.ftc.gov/business-guidance/resources/complying-ftcs-health-breach-notification-rule-0).

### Page 7 - Community cold start, commodity infrastructure, and category confusion

**Concern 1:** Community safety requires moderation, reporting, blocking, crisis procedures, and content density.

**Solution:** Do not launch an open community. Keep client-coach spaces private. Any future cohort feature must first be tested in a facilitated private group on existing software, with a named moderator, rules, reporting, blocking, crisis escalation, misinformation policy, and an explicit rule against exposing disability information. Community is not part of the current investment case.

**Concern 2:** Video, captions, calendar, whiteboard, and transcription are commodities.

**Solution:** Treat them as replaceable infrastructure. Use existing links/services where possible. The product thesis is the assigned-action workflow and its outcome evidence, not media technology.

**Concern 3:** The product may be Patricia's practice rather than scalable software.

**Solution:** The program must include at least eight paying coaches unrelated to the founder or lead coach, with no coach contributing more than 20% of participants. Each coach onboards clients using a documented protocol. FISH passes the independence gate only if at least five external coaches retain clients and achieve acceptable outcomes without manual rescue by Patricia.

**Concern 4:** Consumer app, coaching membership, and coach software have incompatible economics.

**Solution:** Choose coach software. Coaches provide their own coaching; FISH does not include unlimited coach labor. No marketplace take rate, employer sale, or low-cost consumer app appears in the initial model.

### Page 8 - Strongest proposed wedge and MVP

**Assessment recommendation:** between-session execution layer for ADHD/executive-function coaches.

**Revision for the actual product:** preserve the mechanism, narrow the domain to workplace-English communication coaching for neurodivergent professionals.

**Important correction:** the coach assigns the learning action. The client always controls accessibility, notification, consent, disclosure, and pause preferences. Those controls live outside the repeated action flow so the client still returns to one calm next step. "Assigned, never chosen" never overrides bodily autonomy, privacy, or accessibility.

**MVP wording:**

> After a coaching session, the coach assigns one practical workplace-English action. The client returns to one calm screen, completes or responds, and keeps the context with the coach. FISH summarizes facts and barriers without grades, streaks, or shame.

### Page 10 - Sixteen-week validation program

**Response:** Replace the six-week outline with a 16-week program: preparation and discovery, two prospective baseline weeks, a randomized staggered start, eight full intervention weeks per participant, renewal collection, and independent analysis. Retention is measured by elapsed time since each participant activates, never by the program's calendar week alone.

#### Gate 0 - Safety, governance, and protocol readiness, pre-program weeks -2 to 0

Gate 0 is a hard predecessor. No coach/client recruitment, interviews, manual technique test, identifiers, coaching content, or baseline data may be collected until every Gate 0 release item and the independent ethics determination are signed.

- Freeze unvalidated learning features and public community expansion.
- Define one eligible action type for the initial cohort: a coach-assigned workplace email draft with a due date of two to seven days. Use a fixed assignment-quality rubric covering specificity, relevance, difficulty, due date, and client agreement. Other task types are excluded from the primary analysis.
- Complete data inventory, consent copy, access matrix, retention rules, deletion/export path, and incident runbook.
- Stage A registration occurs before recruitment and locks the primary estimand, outcome/denominator rules, assignment cadence, comparison capture method, allocation, effect threshold, non-harm margin, analysis family, stop rules, and a conservative pre-recruitment sample calculation. A research reviewer who did not write the analysis and a privacy reviewer who did not implement the data flow must sign in writing.
- A qualified independent ethics reviewer or counsel must issue a written determination before recruitment. If full ethics review is triggered, recruitment waits for approval; the determination and rationale stay in the data room.

#### Gate 1 - Problem discovery and manual technique proof, weeks 0-2

- Interview at least 15 qualified coaches and 25 clients recruited beyond the founder's personal network. Record inclusion/exclusion, U.S. residence, professional context, compensation, consent, access needs, and saturation.
- Use last-event questions; do not pitch first.
- Pass if at least 60% of coaches describe repeated manual follow-up and at least 50% of clients describe losing or avoiding between-session practice.
- Manually run the exact email-draft assignment protocol with at least ten consenting clients across at least three non-founder coaches. Before automation, require at least 70% tolerability, no severe harm event, coach fidelity above 90% on the assignment-quality rubric, and a directional completion advantage over the client's current method.

#### Gate 2 - Paid sales close, baseline, and allocation, weeks 3-4

- Complete the canonical sales funnel and collect at least eight $297 pilot payments.
- Recruit the powered sample. The conservative Stage A planning target is at least 64 activated clients across at least eight non-founder coaches, with no coach supplying more than 20% of participants and one eligible assignment per participant-week.
- After the two baseline weeks, an independent statistician may perform one Stage B **blinded nuisance-parameter sample-size re-estimation** using pooled baseline rate, coach intraclass correlation, repeated observations, and attrition without access to allocation or treatment outcomes. The primary estimand, 20-point threshold, outcomes, harm margin, alpha 0.05, 80% power, and analysis cannot change. The amendment is time-stamped. If more participants/coaches are required, recruitment and the program end date extend until every added participant completes two baseline weeks and eight intervention weeks. A smaller cohort is feasibility-only and cannot support an institutional outcome claim.
- Collect two full prospective baseline weeks using each client's current method.
- Within each coach, randomize eligible activated clients 1:1 to early or delayed start using a reproducible concealed allocation list generated before assignment. Coaches use the same assignment-quality rubric for both groups. Clients are told when their FISH access begins; no participant is denied normal coaching.
- Capture the primary completion outcome in both arms through the same neutral weekly research form with identical wording, timing, and status definitions, administered independently of FISH and without an extra action reminder. FISH button events are secondary product-usage data, not the cross-arm primary measurement.

#### Gate 3 - Randomized staggered intervention, weeks 5-16

- Weeks 5-8: early-start group uses FISH; delayed-start group continues its current method.
- Weeks 9-12: both groups use FISH. The early group reaches eight intervention weeks at week 12.
- Weeks 13-16: delayed group continues FISH to eight intervention weeks; early group remains observable for a 12-week durability view.
- The primary causal estimand is the intention-to-treat difference in client-reported agreed-action completion between randomized early-start and delayed-start groups during weeks 5-8, accounting for coach clustering and repeated actions. Baseline-adjusted within-person, weeks 9-16, durability, and crossover views are secondary or sensitivity analyses and cannot replace a failed primary contrast.
- Prespecified cadence is exactly one eligible assignment per participant-week. Report (a) end-to-end weekly success, requiring the coach assignment and valid client response; (b) client-response retention conditional on receiving the required assignment; and (c) coach assignment-fidelity. A missed coach assignment fails the end-to-end/product-level metric and the fidelity metric; it never disappears as an ineligible week.
- Report invited-to-activated, invited-to-retained, and activated-to-retained separately. Week-N end-to-end retention requires the prescribed eligible assignment and a valid client response in that elapsed participant week.
- Primary outcome is client-reported agreed-action completion: completed eligible actions divided by all eligible actions due. Missing responses and "Blocked" remain in the intention-to-treat denominator as not completed; "Pause" is reported separately and included in a conservative sensitivity analysis; coach-cancelled or invalid assignments are excluded with reasons. Report intention-to-treat, per-protocol, retained-user, founder-client-excluded, and task-fidelity sensitivity views.
- Success requires an estimated completion lift of at least 20 percentage points and a two-sided 95% confidence interval whose lower bound is above zero. Analyze with a model that accounts for coach clustering and repeated observations. Report coach-stratified results, uncertainty, protocol deviations, missingness, differential dropout, and all adverse findings. If the study is underpowered, describe the result as exploratory even when the point estimate passes.
- Secondary outcomes: coach-confirmed action relevance, fixed-rubric session preparedness, client usefulness, coach chasing/admin minutes, and collected renewal. No English proficiency or confidence claim is permitted.
- Retention gates: at least 70% activation, 60% activated-to-retained at elapsed week 4, and 50% at elapsed week 8. Also report invited-to-retained and 30/60/90-day coach-logo retention.
- External-coach gate: at least five external coaches complete onboarding from written instructions, no coach supplies more than 20% of clients, and external-coach outcomes are no more than ten percentage points below any founder-coach cohort. "Founder rescue" means the founder contacts a client about engagement, rewrites an assignment, troubleshoots outside documented support, or prompts a coach to perform required study behavior; every instance is logged.
- Commercial durability gates: at least six collected renewals, at least 75% gross margin using fully loaded labor, post-onboarding support at or below 15 minutes per coach per week, CAC payback at or below six months, and disclosed refunds/churn.

#### Gate 4 - Harm, renewal, and independent decision, weeks 13-16

- Use a pre-registered three-item trust/burden instrument at baseline, intervention week 2, week 4, week 8, and dropout. Items ask whether FISH increased pressure, felt monitoring-heavy, or made returning harder on a five-point scale. Report item wording, counts, percentages, changes from baseline, missing responses, and coach subgroup.
- The pre-registered non-harm rule uses the mean of the three equally weighted items, where higher is worse. Pass only if the upper bound of the two-sided 95% confidence interval for change from baseline is below +0.5 points and no more than 10% of respondents worsen by two or more points on any item. A worst-case missing-response sensitivity imputes each missing follow-up one category worse than baseline; failure there makes the result inconclusive. The severe-event override applies regardless of the aggregate result.
- Any report of severe distress, coercion, privacy breach, discriminatory output, self-harm risk, or unsafe coach conduct pauses the affected workflow and triggers review by the named safety owner within one business day. The app is not an emergency service; U.S. users are directed to 911/988 where appropriate, and coaches remain responsible for their professional duty of care. FISH documents escalation boundaries in pilot terms.
- Collect recurring-price renewals; stated intent does not count.
- Independent reviewers sign the research analysis, privacy/legal posture, security/access-control results, accessibility report, and financial model. Reviewers cannot be the author or implementer of the artifact they approve. Written dissent and unresolved issues stay in the data room.
- Weeks 13-16 end with a signed decision memo: stop, revise once without expanding scope, or proceed to a 90-day acquisition/retention replication. Institutional fundraising waits for the replication, not merely the first directional cohort.

#### Gate 5 - Fresh 90-day replication before institutional fundraising

- Recruit a fresh cohort through at least two non-founder channels, excluding all prior coaches/clients and founder friends. Use at least eight new paid coaches and the independently powered client count, with no coach contributing more than 20%.
- Offer the same $297 program and $99/month renewal terms. Require eight cleared pilot payments, at least six cleared renewals, and survival through one full recurring cycle.
- Repeat the locked randomized early/delayed primary estimand, identical neutral outcome capture, one-assignment-per-week cadence, denominator rules, non-harm margin, independent analysis, and founder-rescue exclusions. Any protocol improvement is documented and re-registered before recruitment; the original and replication results are both shown.
- Client activated-to-retained thresholds are at least 75% day 30, 65% day 60, and 55% day 90; report invited-to-retained alongside them. Coach-logo retention thresholds are at least 90%, 80%, and 75% at days 30/60/90.
- Repeat commercial gates: at least 75% gross margin, at most 15 support minutes/coach/week after onboarding, CAC payback at most six months, and disclosed refunds/churn.
- Pass only if comparative outcome and non-harm rules replicate, at least five external coaches operate without founder rescue, acquisition works in both channels, and all privacy/security/accessibility gates remain current. Otherwise stop institutional fundraising and issue a stop or scoped-revision memo.

## 9. Measurement and analysis plan

### Primary hypothesis

For the defined U.S. launch segment and one eligible workplace-email action type, FISH increases client-reported agreed-action completion by at least 20 percentage points compared with the client's current method, with the lower bound of the two-sided 95% confidence interval above zero, without a material trust/burden deterioration.

### Primary outcome

**Client-reported agreed-action completion:** eligible actions reported "Done" by the due date on the identical neutral weekly research form used in both randomized arms, divided by all eligible actions due. An eligible action is the one required workplace email draft for that participant-week, agreed by coach and client, scored as passing the assignment-quality rubric, and due in two to seven days. Optional text artifacts and FISH button events support context but do not determine the cross-arm primary outcome.

Denominator rules:

- "Blocked" and missing response: included as not completed in intention-to-treat.
- "Pause": included as not completed in the conservative primary view and reported separately in sensitivity analysis.
- Coach-cancelled, duplicate, technically inaccessible before due date, or rubric-ineligible action: excluded with a coded reason and included in the protocol-deviation appendix.
- Late "Done": reported separately; not primary completion.
- No eligible action issued: counts as failure in the end-to-end/product retention metric and coach-fidelity metric; client-response-conditional retention is reported separately and cannot replace the end-to-end view.

### Secondary outcomes

- Activation within seven days of invitation.
- Invited-to-retained and activated-to-retained at elapsed week 4 and week 8.
- Coach-logo retention at 30, 60, and 90 days and survival of one full recurring billing cycle.
- Coach chasing/admin minutes.
- Coach-confirmed action relevance and fixed-rubric session preparedness.
- Client-rated usefulness.
- Collected pilot payment, collected renewal, refund, and churn.
- Pre-registered pressure, monitoring, and return-difficulty items at baseline, weeks 2/4/8, and dropout.
- Support time and incident rate.
- Assignment fidelity, difficulty rating, and protocol deviations.

These outcomes do not establish English proficiency, communication skill, confidence, emotional regulation, treatment effect, or clinical efficacy.

### Design and analysis

- Two prospective baseline weeks for all participants.
- Randomized, within-coach, concealed 1:1 early/delayed staggered start.
- Primary estimand: intention-to-treat early-start versus delayed-start completion difference during program weeks 5-8. Later shared-intervention and durability periods are secondary.
- Identical neutral weekly research-form outcome capture in both arms; FISH telemetry is secondary.
- A conservative Stage A statistician-approved sample calculation before recruitment, followed only by one blinded nuisance-parameter re-estimation after baseline; any larger requirement extends recruitment and each participant's elapsed clock.
- Primary clustered repeated-measures analysis under intention-to-treat.
- Coach-stratified results and sensitivity views for per-protocol, retained users, founder-client exclusion, assignment fidelity, missingness, and dropout.
- 95% confidence intervals and effect sizes, not thresholds alone.
- No coach contributes more than 20% of the sample.
- Early/delayed contamination, unusual coach contact, outages, and founder rescue are logged.
- Secondary outcomes are labeled exploratory unless separately powered.

### Integrity safeguards

- Time-stamp the protocol, allocation method, metric dictionary, codebook, analysis plan, thresholds, harm items, and stop rules before data collection.
- Preserve allocation and analysis access logs.
- Report every denominator, missing value, dropout, exclusion, protocol deviation, confidence interval, and adverse event.
- Do not discard users who disliked the product.
- Do not change thresholds after seeing results.
- Publish adverse, null, and subgroup findings in the same internal decision package as positive findings.
- Treat thresholds as company decision rules, not universal clinical benchmarks.
- Require an independent statistician to reproduce the primary table from a de-identified export. Require a written pre-recruitment determination by a qualified independent ethics reviewer or counsel and full review if triggered.
