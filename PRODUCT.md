# Product

## Register

product

## Users

FISH serves neurodivergent professionals, including many people with ADHD,
who are learning English with a coach. Clients use the product to follow an
assigned next step and communicate with their coach without navigating a
choice-heavy learning system. Coaches use it to guide clients, assign work,
and maintain one-to-one context.

## Product Purpose

FISH is a calm coaching hub that makes the next useful action obvious. It
supports authentication, client profiles, coach-guided work, and chat while
keeping learning techniques manual until a coach has validated them with real
clients. Success means users can stay focused, understand what happens next,
and return after a gap without judgement.

## Brand Personality

Calm, focused, and supportive. The product speaks plainly, guides without
scolding, and feels dependable rather than attention-seeking.

## Anti-references

FISH must not resemble a noisy course marketplace, a gamified streak tracker,
or a dashboard that exposes every possible option at once. Avoid decorative
SaaS patterns, competing calls to action, alarming error treatments, punitive
progress, plan galleries, and interaction patterns that demand unnecessary
decisions from clients.

## Design Principles

1. Remove choices until one useful next action remains.
2. Let validated coaching practice lead product behavior.
3. Use hierarchy, spacing, and plain language to preserve focus.
4. Reward returning and never punish a gap.
5. Prefer familiar, accessible controls over novel interaction patterns.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Meet WCAG AA as a baseline and design for keyboard use, visible focus,
reduced motion, sufficient contrast in light and dark themes, large 56px touch
targets, readable typography, and calm layout-stable feedback. Never rely on
color alone. Account for attention, working-memory, and cognitive-load needs
through sparse screens, consistent language, and predictable interaction.
