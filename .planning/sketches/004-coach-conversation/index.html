FISH · sketch 004 · coach
Design question

How does a coach correct and assign — without it feeling like grading?

The coach is the one who assigns, so this is where the whole client experience is authored. The coach has a client roster (many clients, not one), and inside a conversation they compose the gentle quoted-reply correction from sketch 002 and assign practice. Three places that authoring can live. Web shows the desktop two-pane; Android the conversation.

D · Synthesis — tap a message → menu → inline compose

Your pick. Tapping the client’s line opens a small menu (fix · praise · assign); choosing Suggest a fix opens the inline compose panel that produces the exact quoted-reply the client receives. Discoverable and branches, with no permanent chrome. It’s live — tap Sofia’s message.

A · Inline compose — tap a message, the composer becomes a fix

The coach taps the client’s line; the composer transforms into a “suggest a fix” panel (the quoted phrase, the corrected version, an optional warm note) and sends it as the quoted-reply correction. Assign lives as a quiet ＋.

B · Message actions — long-press the message for coach tools

The coach long-presses (or hovers) the client’s message; a small menu offers the coach-only actions — suggest a fix, praise, assign practice. Familiar messaging pattern; the tools stay hidden until needed.

C · Coach toolbar — the tools always sit above the composer

A slim coach-only toolbar (correct · assign · voice · praise) lives above the input, always in reach. Fastest for a coach doing many corrections; the cost is one more strip of chrome on every conversation.
