# Sketch 003: Profile

## Design Question

The client's own screen. Because **Progress now has its own tab** (from sketch 001), the profile
doesn't have to repeat it — which frees the real question: how much self-view belongs here, and
how do we show it without a scoreboard? Three framings, each web + Android. All bake in the 001
critique lessons (monochrome, ≥56px targets, no streak, calm).

## How to View

open .planning/sketches/003-profile/index.html

Tap **Appearance** (or the toolbar) to flip light / dark.

## Decision

**Winner: A · Essentials** (user-confirmed 2026-07-04). Because Progress owns its own tab, the
profile stays a calm, low-stakes "you + your coach + preferences" surface — no stats, no
scoreboard. B was rejected (duplicates the Progress tab and edges toward a score); C's warmer
coach card remains available to graft on if desired.

## Variants

- **A · Essentials ★ (selected)** — you, your coach, your settings. No stats (progress lives in the Progress
  tab), so the profile stays a low-stakes, choice-free surface. Calmest.
- **B · Reflection** — adds a reward-only "what you've worked on" block: completed topic chips
  (never a score), current focus, "practicing since March." Warm, but risks duplicating the
  Progress tab and edging toward a scoreboard.
- **C · Relationship** — leads with the coach card (who they are, how long you've worked together,
  one quiet way to reach them), then a compact identity + settings. Warmest and most personal.

## What to Look For

- Does the screen ever read as a **scoreboard**? (The reason B is riskier than A/C.)
- Whether **progress on the profile** duplicates the Progress tab or complements it.
- The **coach relationship** as "assigned, never chosen" — present, warm, not a choice.
- One-primary-action: A/B have none (settings surface); C has a single quiet ghost "Message James".
- Settings tap targets at 56px; Appearance flips the theme live.
