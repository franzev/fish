FISH · sketch 005 · states
Design question

How does the app stay calm when it’s empty, loading, or fails?

The screens designed so far are all full and successful. These are the states a real build needs — and for a neurodivergent audience they must reassure, never alarm. Monochrome, soft notice tone for problems (never red), one calm message, no added choices. Use the cyclers to flip through each state.

A · Message lifecycle — sending → sent → seen → failed

An ADHD client who can’t tell whether a message sent will re-send it. Quiet monochrome status reassures; a failed message uses the soft notice tone with a calm “tap to try again,” never a red error.

B · Empty states — a calm single message, never a dead end

A brand-new client, a day with nothing assigned, a finished journey. Each is one gentle line that explains and reassures (the coach will act), with no buttons demanding a choice.

C · Loading & connection — quiet, never a spinner of doom

Loading shows a soft skeleton of what’s coming (not a blank screen); losing connection shows a calm notice-tone banner that promises messages will send later — never an alarming red failure.
