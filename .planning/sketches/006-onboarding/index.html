Design question

What are a new client’s first five minutes?

After signup, a short data-driven assessment (questions from the database, per the build order) helps the coach tailor things. For an ADHD audience the risk is overload, so how the questions are paced matters. Three ways — and answering questions about yourself is input, not the menu-of-plans the “assigned never chosen” rule forbids.

A · One question per screen — one thing at a time

Full-screen, a single question with big options and a visual progress. The least overload possible; the client only ever sees one decision. Most ADHD-friendly, but more taps to finish.

B · Conversational — the assessment is just a chat

FISH is a ChatHub, so onboarding happens where everything else does: a warm system voice asks one question at a time; the client taps an answer chip and it becomes their reply. No new form paradigm, feels personal and low-pressure, reuses the chat components.

C · Scroll form — a calm single form

All the questions on one gentle scrollable page with progress at the top and one action at the bottom. Fastest to complete for a confident user; the cost is more on screen at once, which is exactly what the audience finds hardest.
