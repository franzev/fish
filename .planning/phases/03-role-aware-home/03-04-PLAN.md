<objective>
Close D-05: a signed-in person visiting `/login` or `/signup` is silently redirected to their role home, so the wrong door (an auth page while already authenticated) opens the right room. Implemented as a small shared server guard applied at each auth route via the standard Next.js server-shell-wraps-client-form pattern — keeping the existing forms' behavior byte-for-byte.

Purpose: The auth pages are the last "wrong doors" in the phase's route map (root, wrong-role home, and now signed-in-visits-auth-page all resolve as silent redirects). This completes the "every mis-navigation resolves silently" contract (D-05) without touching the proven auth form logic.
Output: A `redirectIfSignedIn()` server helper, the login/signup forms extracted into client `*-form.tsx` files, thin server-component route shells that call the guard then render the form, and tests.
</objective>

## Phase Goal

**As a** person who is already logged in, **I want to** be sent to my own home if I ever land back on the login or signup page, **so that** the app never shows me a form I don't need and there is never a wrong door to back out of.
