Close VR-01 by making the shared realtime reconnect lock ownership-safe across conversation changes and proving the exact overlapping A-to-B failure sequence with a deferred-promise regression.

<interfaces>
- `useChatRealtime` owns `seenFirstSubscribeRef: Set<"messages" | "reads" | "reactions">` and `backfillInFlightRef: Promise<void> | null`. The conversation-id effect deliberately resets both so B can establish fresh subscriptions and start recovery without waiting for A.
- `handleReconnected(channelKey)` skips that channel's first SUBSCRIBED callback, returns when `backfillInFlightRef.current` is non-null, then runs `applyGapBackfill ?? refreshConversation`.
- The defect is the current unconditional `backfillInFlightRef.current = null` inside `finally`: after the conversation-id effect releases A's lock slot and B stores its own promise, A can settle late and clear B's entry.
- `chat-client.test.tsx` already exposes `latestSubscribeStatusCallback(channelSuffix)`, uses `rerender` to switch the same mounted ChatClient to another conversation, and uses deferred promises for cross-conversation older-page regressions. Reuse those conventions rather than reaching into hook refs.
- `backfillMessagesAction` receives `{ conversationId, afterCreatedAt, afterMessageId }`; its returned promise keeps `applyGapBackfill` and therefore the realtime lock pending until the test resolves it.
</interfaces>
